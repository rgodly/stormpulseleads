/ The Workflow

From hailstorm to your inbox
in under a day.

Four automated steps. No manual data entry. No waiting on county websites.

/ 01

Storm hits

Our system polls the National Weather Service every 30 minutes. The moment a DFW hailstorm crosses 1″, we know — coordinates, timestamp, intensity.

/ 02

Damage zone mapped

Verified hail report coordinates are overlaid with a precision damage radius. We trace the actual impact boundary — not a rough estimate.

/ 03

Homes matched

County appraisal records are queried for every single-family residence inside the zone. Owner name. Street address. Property class. Appraised value.

/ 04

List delivered

Scored, sorted, geo-clustered. A clean CSV in your inbox — with every lead ranked by damage exposure — while competitors are still reading the weather report.

/ Data Sources

Public records.
Private process.

Three authoritative sources, cross-referenced automatically within minutes of each storm.

Federal

NOAA Storm Reports

Verified hail size, timestamp, and coordinates from official NWS spotters and trained meteorologists. Every report cross-checked against radar data.

noaa.gov · storm_data_reports · real-time feed
County

DCAD + TAD Appraisal Rolls

Dallas and Tarrant Central Appraisal District public property records. Over 1.2M parcels indexed across the metroplex, refreshed each appraisal cycle.

dcad.org · tad.org · ~1.2M parcels indexed
Geospatial

County GIS Boundaries

Parcel-level geographic data from county GIS portals used to precisely intersect storm damage polygons with property boundaries — no street-level guessing.

Collin · Denton · Rockwall · Johnson GIS data
